Introduction

In 2009, the first form of cryptocurrency was created with "Bitcoin". Crypto assets use blockchain technology, a type of digital ledger, to record all transactions across a large network of computers. Crypto is usually 'mined' with its input resources usually being large amounts of computational power. This differs from traditional currencies such as the USD, Euro, or stock assets as crypto is disconnected from any government entity or centralized bank. These coins hold little physical value, but work similarly to traditional stocks.

Now in 2025, thousands of cryptocurrencies have been created, each with its own valuation and branding. Prices fluctuate throughout the day, and savvy investors may be able to capitalize at higher returns than traditional shares purchased on the NASDAQ. Our project aims to compare the 2 investments, traditional stock and the newer cryptocurrencies, and determine the pros and cons to each. We hope to examine volatility, average returns on investment, and visualize the many different types of coins and companies. We believe cryptocurrency is the future of investment, and believe the topic is extremely important with growing concerns in the U.S stock markets.

Conclusion

In conclusion, our analysis of cryptocurrencies and traditional stocks reveals distinct characteristics and investment potentials for each asset class. Cryptocurrencies offer high volatility and the possibility of significant returns, appealing to risk-tolerant investors. However, they also come with regulatory uncertainties and security concerns. On the other hand, traditional stocks provide more stability and are backed by established companies, making them a safer choice for conservative investors. Our visualizations highlighted these differences, showcasing the rapid price fluctuations of cryptocurrencies compared to the steadier growth of stocks. Ultimately, the choice between investing in cryptocurrencies or traditional stocks depends on individual risk tolerance, investment goals, and market outlook. Using recent data, we also found that aggregate cryptocurrency has generally outperformed common stocks since the initial tariffs in April. This confirms our hypothesis that since crypto is not a taxable good, it would feel less impact from Trump's changes. We also noticed that Crypto tracks the market. It follows the general flow of the S&P 500 and even closer with the technology sector. While this may be a short term coincidence, the correlation is strong and suggests investors value both types of assets. By examining the different in cryptocurrency performance during highly volatile times, we can see if the blockchain assets really are safer investments when the global economy enters limbo. With these comparisons we hope to make a fuller picture of crypto and common stock markets for new investors.
