Introduction

In 2009, the first form of cryptocurrency was created with "Bitcoin". Crypto assets use blockchain technology, a type of digital ledger, to record all transactions across a large network of computers. Crypto is usually 'mined' with its input resources usually being large amounts of computational power. This differs from traditional currencies such as the USD, Euro, or stock assets as crypto is disconnected from any government entity or centralized bank. These coins hold little physical value, but work similarly to traditional stocks.

Now in 2025, thousands of cryptocurrencies have been created, each with its own valuation and branding. Prices fluctuate throughout the day, and savvy investors may be able to capitalize at higher returns than traditional shares purchased on the NASDAQ. Our project aims to compare the 2 investments, traditional stock and the newer cryptocurrencies, and determine the pros and cons to each. We hope to examine volatility, average returns on investment, and visualize the many different types of coins and companies. We believe cryptocurrency is the future of investment, and believe the topic is extremely important with growing concerns in the U.S stock markets.

Visualization 1

This chart shows a treemap visualization comparing the market capitalization of various stocks and cryptocurrencies. Each rectangle represents an individual asset, with the size of the rectangle corresponding to its market cap. The color coding differentiates between stocks (yellow) and cryptocurrencies (pink). From the visualization, we can see that while traditional stocks dominate in terms of market capitalization, certain cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum also hold significant market value. Visualization 2

This visualization illustrates how the stock market behaves on a day-to-day basis by tracking the average percent change of stock prices over time. When viewed across multiple months, the chart reveals that most daily changes remain clustered close to zero, meaning that the market tends to move gradually rather than through extreme daily swings. The few noticeable spikes and dips correspond to periods of unusual volatility—likely earnings releases, macroeconomic news, or shifts in investor sentiment—which briefly disrupt the otherwise stable trend. The interactive time filters (1W, 2W, 1M, All) allow users to zoom into shorter windows and uncover short-term patterns, such as momentum surges, brief pullbacks, or stabilization phases. Together, these insights help contextualize how dynamic the market is, making it easier for viewers to understand whether recent price movements are typical fluctuations or indicators of emerging trends.

Visualization 4

Across both charts, April's market behavior shows a stark divide between traditional stocks and cryptocurrencies. The stock movers display a tight cluster of modest changes, with most companies shifting less than 1% in either direction—highlighting the stability and predictability of the equity market during this period. In contrast, the crypto-only chart reveals dramatic volatility, with many tokens experiencing explosive gains ranging from 30% to over 200%. Viewed together, the visualizations emphasize how stocks and cryptocurrencies operate on entirely different volatility scales, with equities moving gradually while crypto assets can swing wildly within the same timeframe.

Visualization 5

This bubble plot highlights the relationship between price, average percent change, and trading volume across the most actively traded stocks and cryptocurrencies over the past 30 days. The visualization makes the differences between the two asset classes immediately apparent: cryptocurrencies exhibit much wider variation in both price and volatility, forming a dispersed cloud of points across the chart. Stocks, on the other hand, cluster tightly around lower levels of average percent change, demonstrating far more stability in their monthly performance. Larger bubbles—representing higher trading volume—help viewers pinpoint which assets are driving market activity, showing that some extremely volatile crypto assets also receive substantial trading attention. By combining three dimensions of data into a single chart, this visualization uncovers patterns that would be harder to detect when viewing these variables separately, making it clear how volatility and market behavior differ dramatically between stocks and crypto.

Conclusion

In conclusion, our analysis of cryptocurrencies and traditional stocks reveals distinct characteristics and investment potentials for each asset class. Cryptocurrencies offer high volatility and the possibility of significant returns, appealing to risk-tolerant investors. However, they also come with regulatory uncertainties and security concerns. On the other hand, traditional stocks provide more stability and are backed by established companies, making them a safer choice for conservative investors. Our visualizations highlighted these differences, showcasing the rapid price fluctuations of cryptocurrencies compared to the steadier growth of stocks. Ultimately, the choice between investing in cryptocurrencies or traditional stocks depends on individual risk tolerance, investment goals, and market outlook.

Using recent data, we also found that aggregate cryptocurrency has generally outperformed common stocks since the initial tariffs in April. This confirms our hypothesis that since crypto is not a taxable good, it would feel less impact from Trump's changes. We also noticed that Crypto tracks the market. It follows the general flow of the S&P 500 and even closer with the technology sector.
